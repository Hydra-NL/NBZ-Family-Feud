How to play

Rules & Good-to-Knows

  • Consulting with the team is not allowed, unless told otherwise
  • No team is allowed to use any external sources for the game
  • An answer must be given within a reasonable time (aprox. 10s)
  • The tech-team has the final verdict on whether a question was answered correctly or not

How to play

The game is played in two teams that face off against each other. At the start of the game the team-leader is the first one to get the opportunity to answer a question.

After that the turn gets passed to the one next to the team-leader and so on. Until everyone has answered a question, then the turn goes back to the team-leader.

Face-off

It starts with a 'face-off' question. This question decides who gets to choose which team starts the game.

The first team to answer the question correctly wins the face-off and gets to choose to either play the question or pass it to the opposite team.

Passing a question

If the team chooses to pass the question, the other team gets to go first in an attempt to clear the board.


Playing a question

If the team chooses to play the question, it's their goal to clear the board of all the answers.

Giving an incorrect answer, or failing to respond in time, will result in a STRIKE.

If the team gets three(3) STRIKEs before clearing the board, the opposite team will now get the chance to steal the question.

Stealing a question

The team can now discuss with each other for 30 seconds.

Once they all agree to an answer the Team leader gives the answer to the host.

If the answer given is on the board their team will steal ALL the points earned by the opposite team in this round.

If an incorrect answer is given, the team that was originally in control of the question keeps their points.

Points

Points are based on the amount of people who gave the same reponse to the survey.

[EXAMPLE]:
Name a red fruit...
46 people responded with 'Apple', that answer will be worth 46 points.
